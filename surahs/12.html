alif-Lam-ra الٓر Alif Laam Ra til'ka تِلْكَ These āyātu ءَايَاتُ (are the) Verses l-kitābi الْكِتَابِ (of) the Book l-mubīni الْمُبِينِ [the] clear

1

1.

Alif, Lam, Ra. These are the verses of the clear Book.

1

innā إِنَّا Indeed, We anzalnāhu أَنْزَلْنَاهُ We have sent it down qur'ānan قُرْءَانًا (as) a Quran in Arabic ʿarabiyyan عَرَبِيًّا (as) a Quran in Arabic laʿallakum لَعَلَّكُمْ so that you may taʿqilūna تَعْقِلُونَ understand

2

2.

Indeed, We have sent it down as an Arabic Qur'an that you might understand.

2

naḥnu نَحْنُ We naquṣṣu نَقُصُّ relate ʿalayka عَلَيْكَ to you aḥsana أَحْسَنَ the best l-qaṣaṣi الْقَصَصِ of the narrations bimā بِمَا in what awḥaynā أَوْحَيْنَا We have revealed ilayka إِلَيْكَ to you hādhā هَذَا (of) this l-qur'āna الْقُرْءَانَ the Quran wa-in وَإِنْ although kunta كُنْتَ you were min مِنْ before it qablihi قَبْلِهِ before it lamina لَمِنَ surely among l-ghāfilīna الْغَافِلِينَ the unaware

3

3.

We relate to you, [O Muhammad], the best of stories in what We have revealed to you of this Qur'an although you were, before it, among the unaware.

3

idh إِذْ When qāla قَالَ said yūsufu يُوسُفُ Yusuf li-abīhi لِأَبِيهِ to his father yāabati يَاأَبَتِ O my father innī إِنِّي Indeed, I ra-aytu رَأَيْتُ I saw aḥada أَحَدَ eleven ʿashara عَشَرَ eleven kawkaban كَوْكَبًا star(s) wal-shamsa وَالشَّمْسَ and the sun wal-qamara وَالْقَمَرَ and the moon ra-aytuhum رَأَيْتُهُمْ I saw them lī لِي to me sājidīna سَاجِدِينَ prostrating

4

4.

[Of these stories mention] when Joseph said to his father, "O my father, indeed I have seen [in a dream] eleven stars and the sun and the moon; I saw them prostrating to me."

4

qāla قَالَ He said yābunayya يَابُنَيَّ O my son! lā لَا (Do) not taqṣuṣ تَقْصُصْ relate ru'yāka رُءْيَاكَ your vision ʿalā عَلَى to ikh'watika إِخْوَتِكَ your brothers fayakīdū فَيَكِيدُوا lest they plan laka لَكَ against you kaydan كَيْدًا a plot inna إِنَّ Indeed l-shayṭāna الشَّيْطَانَ the Shaitaan lil'insāni لِلْإِنْسَانِ (is) to man ʿaduwwun عَدُوٌّ an enemy mubīnun مُبِينٌ open

5

5.

He said, "O my son, do not relate your vision to your brothers or they will contrive against you a plan. Indeed Satan, to man, is a manifest enemy.

5

wakadhālika وَكَذَلِكَ And thus yajtabīka يَجْتَبِيكَ will choose you rabbuka رَبُّكَ your Lord wayuʿallimuka وَيُعَلِّمُكَ and will teach you min مِنْ of tawīli تَأْوِيلِ (the) interpretation l-aḥādīthi الْأَحَادِيثِ (of) the narratives wayutimmu وَيُتِمُّ and complete niʿ'matahu نِعْمَتَهُ His Favor ʿalayka عَلَيْكَ on you waʿalā وَعَلَى and on āli ءَالِ (the) family yaʿqūba يَعْقُوبَ (of) Yaqub kamā كَمَا as atammahā أَتَمَّهَا He completed it ʿalā عَلَى on abawayka أَبَوَيْكَ your two forefathers min مِنْ before qablu قَبْلُ before ib'rāhīma إِبْرَاهِيمَ Ibrahim wa-is'ḥāqa وَإِسْحَاقَ and Ishaq inna إِنَّ Indeed rabbaka رَبَّكَ your Lord ʿalīmun عَلِيمٌ (is) All-Knower ḥakīmun حَكِيمٌ All-Wise

6

6.

And thus will your Lord choose you and teach you the interpretation of narratives and complete His favor upon you and upon the family of Jacob, as He completed it upon your fathers before, Abraham and Isaac. Indeed, your Lord is Knowing and Wise."

6

laqad لَقَدْ Certainly kāna كَانَ were fī فِي in yūsufa يُوسُفَ Yusuf wa-ikh'watihi وَإِخْوَتِهِ and his brothers āyātun ءَايَاتٌ signs lilssāilīna لِلسَّائِلِينَ for those who ask

7

7.

Certainly were there in Joseph and his brothers signs for those who ask,

7

idh إِذْ When qālū قَالُوا they said layūsufu لَيُوسُفُ Surely Yusuf wa-akhūhu وَأَخُوهُ and his brother aḥabbu أَحَبُّ (are) more beloved ilā إِلَى to abīnā أَبِينَا our father minnā مِنَّا than we wanaḥnu وَنَحْنُ while we ʿuṣ'batun عُصْبَةٌ (are) a group inna إِنَّ Indeed abānā أَبَانَا our father lafī لَفِي (is) surely in ḍalālin ضَلَالٍ an error mubīnin مُبِينٍ clear

8

8.

When they said, "Joseph and his brother are more beloved to our father than we, while we are a clan. Indeed, our father is in clear error.

8

uq'tulū اقْتُلُوا Kill yūsufa يُوسُفَ Yusuf awi أَوِ or iṭ'raḥūhu اطْرَحُوهُ cast him arḍan أَرْضًا (to) a land yakhlu يَخْلُ so will be free lakum لَكُمْ for you wajhu وَجْهُ (the) face abīkum أَبِيكُمْ (of) your father watakūnū وَتَكُونُوا and you will be min مِنْ after that baʿdihi بَعْدِهِ after that qawman قَوْمًا a people ṣāliḥīna صَالِحِينَ righteous

9

9.

Kill Joseph or cast him out to [another] land; the countenance of your father will [then] be only for you, and you will be after that a righteous people."

9

qāla قَالَ Said qāilun قَائِلٌ a speaker min'hum مِنْهُمْ among them lā لَا (Do) not taqtulū تَقْتُلُوا kill yūsufa يُوسُفَ Yusuf wa-alqūhu وَأَلْقُوهُ but throw him fī فِي in ghayābati غَيَبَتِ the bottom l-jubi الْجُبِّ (of) the well yaltaqiṭ'hu يَلْتَقِطْهُ will pick him baʿḍu بَعْضُ some l-sayārati السَّيَّارَةِ [the] caravan in إِنْ if kuntum كُنْتُمْ you are fāʿilīna فَاعِلِينَ doing

10

10.

Said a speaker among them, "Do not kill Joseph but throw him into the bottom of the well; some travelers will pick him up - if you would do [something]."

10

qālū قَالُوا They said yāabānā يَاأَبَانَا O our father! mā مَا Why laka لَكَ (do) you lā لَا not tamannā تَأْمَنَّا trust us ʿalā عَلَى with yūsufa يُوسُفَ Yusuf wa-innā وَإِنَّا while indeed we lahu لَهُ (are) for him lanāṣiḥūna لَنَاصِحُونَ surely well-wishers

11

11.

They said, "O our father, why do you not entrust us with Joseph while indeed, we are to him sincere counselors?

11

arsil'hu أَرْسِلْهُ Send him maʿanā مَعَنَا with us ghadan غَدًا tomorrow yartaʿ يَرْتَعْ (to) enjoy wayalʿab وَيَلْعَبْ and play wa-innā وَإِنَّا And indeed, we lahu لَهُ for him laḥāfiẓūna لَحَافِظُونَ (will) surely (be) guardians

12

12.

Send him with us tomorrow that he may eat well and play. And indeed, we will be his guardians.

12

qāla قَالَ He said innī إِنِّي Indeed, [I] layaḥzununī لَيَحْزُنُنِي it surely saddens me an أَنْ that tadhhabū تَذْهَبُوا you should take him bihi بِهِ you should take him wa-akhāfu وَأَخَافُ and I fear an أَنْ that yakulahu يَأْكُلَهُ would eat him l-dhi'bu الذِّئْبُ a wolf wa-antum وَأَنْتُمْ while you ʿanhu عَنْهُ of him ghāfilūna غَافِلُونَ (are) unaware

13

13.

[Jacob] said, "Indeed, it saddens me that you should take him, and I fear that a wolf would eat him while you are of him unaware."

13

qālū قَالُوا They said la-in لَئِنْ If akalahu أَكَلَهُ eats him l-dhi'bu الذِّئْبُ the wolf wanaḥnu وَنَحْنُ while we ʿuṣ'batun عُصْبَةٌ (are) a group innā إِنَّا indeed we idhan إِذًا then lakhāsirūna لَخَاسِرُونَ surely (would be) losers

14

14.

They said, "If a wolf should eat him while we are a [strong] clan, indeed, we would then be losers."

14

falammā فَلَمَّا So when dhahabū ذَهَبُوا they took him bihi بِهِ they took him wa-ajmaʿū وَأَجْمَعُوا and agreed an أَنْ that yajʿalūhu يَجْعَلُوهُ they put him fī فِي in ghayābati غَيَبَتِ (the) bottom l-jubi الْجُبِّ (of) the well wa-awḥaynā وَأَوْحَيْنَا But We inspired ilayhi إِلَيْهِ to him latunabbi-annahum لَتُنَبِّئَنَّهُمْ Surely, you will inform them bi-amrihim بِأَمْرِهِمْ about this affair hādhā هَذَا about this affair wahum وَهُمْ while they lā لَا (do) not yashʿurūna يَشْعُرُونَ perceive

15

15.

So when they took him [out] and agreed to put him into the bottom of the well... But We inspired to him, "You will surely inform them [someday] about this affair of theirs while they do not perceive [your identity]."

15

wajāū وَجَاءُو And they came abāhum أَبَاهُمْ (to) their father ʿishāan عِشَاءً early at night yabkūna يَبْكُونَ weeping

16

16.

And they came to their father at night, weeping.

16

qālū قَالُوا They said yāabānā يَاأَبَانَا O our father! innā إِنَّا Indeed we dhahabnā ذَهَبْنَا [we] went nastabiqu نَسْتَبِقُ racing each other wataraknā وَتَرَكْنَا and we left yūsufa يُوسُفَ Yusuf ʿinda عِنْدَ with matāʿinā مَتَاعِنَا our possessions fa-akalahu فَأَكَلَهُ and ate him l-dhi'bu الذِّئْبُ the wolf wamā وَمَا But not anta أَنْتَ you bimu'minin بِمُؤْمِنٍ (will) believe lanā لَنَا us walaw وَلَوْ even if kunnā كُنَّا we are ṣādiqīna صَادِقِينَ truthful

17

17.

They said, "O our father, indeed we went racing each other and left Joseph with our possessions, and a wolf ate him. But you would not believe us, even if we were truthful."

17

wajāū وَجَاءُو And they brought ʿalā عَلَى upon qamīṣihi قَمِيصِهِ his shirt bidamin بِدَمٍ with false blood kadhibin كَذِبٍ with false blood qāla قَالَ He said bal بَلْ Nay sawwalat سَوَّلَتْ has enticed you lakum لَكُمْ has enticed you anfusukum أَنْفُسُكُمْ your souls amran أَمْرًا (to) a matter faṣabrun فَصَبْرٌ so patience jamīlun جَمِيلٌ (is) beautiful wal-lahu وَاللَّهُ And Allah l-mus'taʿānu الْمُسْتَعَانُ (is) the One sought for help ʿalā عَلَى against mā مَا what taṣifūna تَصِفُونَ you describe

18

18.

And they brought upon his shirt false blood. [Jacob] said, "Rather, your souls have enticed you to something, so patience is most fitting. And Allah is the one sought for help against that which you describe."

18

wajāat وَجَاءَتْ And there came sayyāratun سَيَّارَةٌ a caravan fa-arsalū فَأَرْسَلُوا and they sent wāridahum وَارِدَهُمْ their water drawer fa-adlā فَأَدْلَى then he let down dalwahu دَلْوَهُ his bucket qāla قَالَ He said yābush'rā يَابُشْرَى O good news! hādhā هَذَا This ghulāmun غُلَامٌ (is) a boy wa-asarrūhu وَأَسَرُّوهُ And they hid him biḍāʿatan بِضَاعَةً (as) a merchandise wal-lahu وَاللَّهُ And Allah ʿalīmun عَلِيمٌ (is) All- Knower bimā بِمَا of what yaʿmalūna يَعْمَلُونَ they do

19

19.

And there came a company of travelers; then they sent their water drawer, and he let down his bucket. He said, "Good news! Here is a boy." And they concealed him, [taking him] as merchandise; and Allah was knowing of what they did.

19

washarawhu وَشَرَوْهُ And they sold him bithamanin بِثَمَنٍ for a price bakhsin بَخْسٍ very low darāhima دَرَاهِمَ dirhams maʿdūdatin مَعْدُودَةٍ few wakānū وَكَانُوا and they were fīhi فِيهِ about him mina مِنَ of l-zāhidīna الزَّاهِدِينَ those keen to give up

20

20.

And they sold him for a reduced price - a few dirhams - and they were, concerning him, of those content with little.

20

waqāla وَقَالَ And said alladhī الَّذِي the one who ish'tarāhu اشْتَرَاهُ bought him min مِنْ of miṣ'ra مِصْرَ Egypt li-im'ra-atihi لِامْرَأَتِهِ to his wife akrimī أَكْرِمِي Make comfortable mathwāhu مَثْوَاهُ his stay ʿasā عَسَى Perhaps an أَنْ that yanfaʿanā يَنْفَعَنَا (he) will benefit us aw أَوْ or nattakhidhahu نَتَّخِذَهُ we will take him waladan وَلَدًا (as) a son wakadhālika وَكَذَلِكَ And thus makkannā مَكَّنَّا We established liyūsufa لِيُوسُفَ Yusuf fī فِي in l-arḍi الْأَرْضِ the land walinuʿallimahu وَلِنُعَلِّمَهُ that We might teach him min مِنْ (the) interpretation of tawīli تَأْوِيلِ (the) interpretation of l-aḥādīthi الْأَحَادِيثِ the events wal-lahu وَاللَّهُ And Allah ghālibun غَالِبٌ (is) Predominant ʿalā عَلَى over amrihi أَمْرِهِ His affairs walākinna وَلَكِنَّ but akthara أَكْثَرَ most l-nāsi النَّاسِ (of) the people lā لَا (do) not yaʿlamūna يَعْلَمُونَ know

21

21.

And the one from Egypt who bought him said to his wife, "Make his residence comfortable. Perhaps he will benefit us, or we will adopt him as a son." And thus, We established Joseph in the land that We might teach him the interpretation of events. And Allah is predominant over His affair, but most of the people do not know.

21

walammā وَلَمَّا And when balagha بَلَغَ he reached ashuddahu أَشُدَّهُ his maturity ātaynāhu ءَاتَيْنَاهُ We gave him ḥuk'man حُكْمًا wisdom waʿil'man وَعِلْمًا and knowledge wakadhālika وَكَذَلِكَ And thus najzī نَجْزِي We reward l-muḥ'sinīna الْمُحْسِنِينَ the good-doers

22

22.

And when Joseph reached maturity, We gave him judgment and knowledge. And thus We reward the doers of good.

22

warāwadathu وَرَاوَدَتْهُ And sought to seduce him allatī الَّتِي she who huwa هُوَ he (was) fī فِي in baytihā بَيْتِهَا her house ʿan عَنْ from nafsihi نَفْسِهِ his self waghallaqati وَغَلَّقَتِ And she closed l-abwāba الْأَبْوَابَ the doors waqālat وَقَالَتْ and she said hayta هَيْتَ Come on laka لَكَ you qāla قَالَ He said maʿādha مَعَاذَ I seek refuge in Allah l-lahi اللَّهِ I seek refuge in Allah innahu إِنَّهُ Indeed, he rabbī رَبِّي (is) my lord aḥsana أَحْسَنَ (who has) made good mathwāya مَثْوَايَ my stay innahu إِنَّهُ Indeed lā لَا not yuf'liḥu يُفْلِحُ will succeed l-ẓālimūna الظَّالِمُونَ the wrongdoers

23

23.
