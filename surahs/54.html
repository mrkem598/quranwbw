iq'tarabati اقْتَرَبَتِ Has come near l-sāʿatu السَّاعَةُ the Hour wa-inshaqqa وَانْشَقَّ and has split l-qamaru الْقَمَرُ the moon

1

1.

The Hour has come near, and the moon has split [in two].

1

wa-in وَإِنْ And if yaraw يَرَوْا they see āyatan ءَايَةً a Sign yuʿ'riḍū يُعْرِضُوا they turn away wayaqūlū وَيَقُولُوا and say siḥ'run سِحْرٌ Magic mus'tamirrun مُسْتَمِرٌّ continuing

2

2.

And if they see a miracle, they turn away and say, "Passing magic."

2

wakadhabū وَكَذَّبُوا And they denied wa-ittabaʿū وَاتَّبَعُوا and followed ahwāahum أَهْوَاءَهُمْ their desires wakullu وَكُلُّ but (for) every amrin أَمْرٍ matter mus'taqirrun مُسْتَقِرٌّ (will be a) settlement

3

3.

And they denied and followed their inclinations. But for every matter is a [time of] settlement.

3

walaqad وَلَقَدْ And certainly jāahum جَاءَهُمْ has come to them mina مِنَ of l-anbāi الْأَنْبَاءِ the information mā مَا wherein fīhi فِيهِ wherein muz'dajarun مُزْدَجَرٌ (is) deterrence

4

4.

And there has already come to them of information that in which there is deterrence -

4

ḥik'matun حِكْمَةٌ Wisdom bālighatun بَالِغَةٌ perfect famā فَمَا but not tugh'ni تُغْنِ will avail l-nudhuru النُّذُرُ the warnings

5

5.

Extensive wisdom - but warning does not avail [them].

5

fatawalla فَتَوَلَّ So turn away ʿanhum عَنْهُمْ from them yawma يَوْمَ (The) Day yadʿu يَدْعُ will call l-dāʿi الدَّاعِ the caller ilā إِلَى to shayin شَيْءٍ a thing nukurin نُكُرٍ terrible

6

6.

So leave them, [O Muhammad]. The Day the Caller calls to something forbidding,

6

khushaʿan خُشَّعًا (Will be) humbled abṣāruhum أَبْصَارُهُمْ their eyes yakhrujūna يَخْرُجُونَ they will come forth mina مِنَ from l-ajdāthi الْأَجْدَاثِ the graves ka-annahum كَأَنَّهُمْ as if they (were) jarādun جَرَادٌ locusts muntashirun مُنْتَشِرٌ spreading

7

7.

Their eyes humbled, they will emerge from the graves as if they were locusts spreading,

7

muh'ṭiʿīna مُهْطِعِينَ Racing ahead ilā إِلَى toward l-dāʿi الدَّاعِ the caller yaqūlu يَقُولُ Will say l-kāfirūna الْكَافِرُونَ the disbelievers hādhā هَذَا This yawmun يَوْمٌ (is) a Day ʿasirun عَسِرٌ difficult

8

8.

Racing ahead toward the Caller. The disbelievers will say, "This is a difficult Day."

8

9

9.

The people of Noah denied before them, and they denied Our servant and said, "A madman," and he was repelled.

9

fadaʿā فَدَعَا So he called rabbahu رَبَّهُ his Lord annī أَنِّي I am maghlūbun مَغْلُوبٌ one overpowered fa-intaṣir فَانْتَصِرْ so help

10

10.

So he invoked his Lord, "Indeed, I am overpowered, so help."

11

11.

Then We opened the gates of the heaven with rain pouring down

12

12.

And caused the earth to burst with springs, and the waters met for a matter already predestined.

Sailing under Our observation as reward for he who had been denied.

15

15.

And We left it as a sign, so is there any who will remember?

19

19.

Indeed, We sent upon them a screaming wind on a day of continuous misfortune,

tanziʿu تَنْزِعُ Plucking out l-nāsa النَّاسَ men ka-annahum كَأَنَّهُمْ as if they (were) aʿjāzu أَعْجَازُ trunks nakhlin نَخْلٍ (of) date-palms munqaʿirin مُنْقَعِرٍ uprooted
