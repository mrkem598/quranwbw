waL-nāziʿāti وَالنَّازِعَاتِ By those who extract gharqan غَرْقًا violently

1

1.

By those [angels] who extract with violence

1

wal-nāshiṭāti وَالنَّاشِطَاتِ And those who draw out nashṭan نَشْطًا gently

2

2.

And [by] those who remove with ease

2

wal-sābiḥāti وَالسَّابِحَاتِ And those who glide sabḥan سَبْحًا swimming

3

3.

And [by] those who glide [as if] swimming

3

fal-sābiqāti فَالسَّابِقَاتِ And those who race each other sabqan سَبْقًا (in) a race

4

4.

And those who race each other in a race

4

fal-mudabirāti فَالْمُدَبِّرَاتِ And those who arrange amran أَمْرًا (the) matter

5

5.

And those who arrange [each] matter,

5

yawma يَوْمَ (The) Day tarjufu تَرْجُفُ will quake l-rājifatu الرَّاجِفَةُ the quaking one

6

6.

On the Day the blast [of the Horn] will convulse [creation],

6

tatbaʿuhā تَتْبَعُهَا Follows it l-rādifatu الرَّادِفَةُ the subsequent

7

7.

There will follow it the subsequent [one].

7

qulūbun قُلُوبٌ Hearts yawma-idhin يَوْمَئِذٍ that Day wājifatun وَاجِفَةٌ will palpitate

8

8.

Hearts, that Day, will tremble,

8

abṣāruhā أَبْصَارُهَا Their eyes khāshiʿatun خَاشِعَةٌ humbled

9

9.

Their eyes humbled.

9

yaqūlūna يَقُولُونَ They say a-innā أَءِنَّا Will we lamardūdūna لَمَرْدُودُونَ indeed be returned fī فِي to l-ḥāfirati الْحَافِرَةِ the former state

10

10.

They are [presently] saying, "Will we indeed be returned to [our] former state [of life]?

10

a-idhā أَءِذَا What! When kunnā كُنَّا we are ʿiẓāman عِظَامًا bones nakhiratan نَخِرَةً decayed

11

11.

Even if we should be decayed bones?

11

qālū قَالُوا They say til'ka تِلْكَ This idhan إِذًا then karratun كَرَّةٌ (would be) a return khāsiratun خَاسِرَةٌ losing

12

12.

They say, "That, then, would be a losing return."

12

fa-innamā فَإِنَّمَا Then only hiya هِيَ it zajratun زَجْرَةٌ (will be) a shout wāḥidatun وَاحِدَةٌ single

13

13.

Indeed, it will be but one shout,

13

fa-idhā فَإِذَا And behold! hum هُمْ They bil-sāhirati بِالسَّاهِرَةِ (will be) awakened

14

14.

And suddenly they will be [alert] upon the earth's surface.

14

hal هَلْ Has atāka أَتَىكَ (there) come to you ḥadīthu حَدِيثُ (the) story mūsā مُوسَى (of) Musa

15

15.

Has there reached you the story of Moses? -

15

idh إِذْ When nādāhu نَادَاهُ called him rabbuhu رَبُّهُ his Lord bil-wādi بِالْوَادِ in the valley l-muqadasi الْمُقَدَّسِ the sacred ṭuwan طُوًى (of) Tuwa

16

16.

When his Lord called to him in the sacred valley of Tuwa,

16

idh'hab اذْهَبْ Go ilā إِلَى to fir'ʿawna فِرْعَوْنَ Firaun innahu إِنَّهُ Indeed he ṭaghā طَغَى (has) transgressed

17

17.

"Go to Pharaoh. Indeed, he has transgressed.

17

faqul فَقُلْ And say hal هَلْ Would laka لَكَ [for] you ilā إِلَى [until] an أَنْ [that] tazakkā تَزَكَّى purify yourself

18

18.

And say to him, 'Would you [be willing to] purify yourself

18

wa-ahdiyaka وَأَهْدِيَكَ And I will guide you ilā إِلَى to rabbika رَبِّكَ your Lord fatakhshā فَتَخْشَى so you would fear

19

19.

And let me guide you to your Lord so you would fear [Him]?'"

19

fa-arāhu فَأَرَاهُ Then he showed him l-āyata الْءَايَةَ the sign l-kub'rā الْكُبْرَى the great

20

20.

And he showed him the greatest sign,

20

fakadhaba فَكَذَّبَ But he denied waʿaṣā وَعَصَى and disobeyed

21

21.

But Pharaoh denied and disobeyed.

21

thumma ثُمَّ Then adbara أَدْبَرَ he turned his back yasʿā يَسْعَى striving

22

22.

Then he turned his back, striving.

22

faḥashara فَحَشَرَ And he gathered fanādā فَنَادَى and called out

23

23.

And he gathered [his people] and called out

23

faqāla فَقَالَ Then he said anā أَنَا I am rabbukumu رَبُّكُمُ your Lord l-aʿlā الْأَعْلَى the Most High

24

24.

And said, "I am your most exalted lord."

24

fa-akhadhahu فَأَخَذَهُ So seized him l-lahu اللَّهُ Allah nakāla نَكَالَ (with) an exemplary punishment l-ākhirati الْءَاخِرَةِ (for) the last wal-ūlā وَالْأُولَى and the first

25

25.

So Allah seized him in exemplary punishment for the last and the first [transgression].

25

inna إِنَّ Indeed fī فِي in dhālika ذَلِكَ that laʿib'ratan لَعِبْرَةً surely (is) a lesson liman لِمَنْ for whoever yakhshā يَخْشَى fears

26

26.

Indeed in that is a warning for whoever would fear [Allah].

26

a-antum ءَأَنْتُمْ Are you ashaddu أَشَدُّ a more difficult khalqan خَلْقًا creation ami أَمِ or l-samāu السَّمَاءُ the heaven banāhā بَنَاهَا He constructed it

27

27.

Are you a more difficult creation or is the heaven? Allah constructed it.

27

rafaʿa رَفَعَ He raised samkahā سَمْكَهَا its ceiling fasawwāhā فَسَوَّاهَا and proportioned it

28

28.

He raised its ceiling and proportioned it.

28

wa-aghṭasha وَأَغْطَشَ And He darkened laylahā لَيْلَهَا its night wa-akhraja وَأَخْرَجَ and brought out ḍuḥāhā ضُحَاهَا its brightness

29

29.

And He darkened its night and extracted its brightness.

29

wal-arḍa وَالْأَرْضَ And the earth baʿda بَعْدَ after dhālika ذَلِكَ that daḥāhā دَحَاهَا He spread it

30

30.

And after that He spread the earth.

30

akhraja أَخْرَجَ He brought forth min'hā مِنْهَا from it māahā مَاءَهَا its water wamarʿāhā وَمَرْعَاهَا and its pasture

31

31.

He extracted from it its water and its pasture,

31

wal-jibāla وَالْجِبَالَ And the mountains arsāhā أَرْسَاهَا He made them firm

32

32.

And the mountains He set firmly

32

matāʿan مَتَاعًا (As) a provision lakum لَكُمْ for you wali-anʿāmikum وَلِأَنْعَامِكُمْ and for your cattle

33

33.

As provision for you and your grazing livestock.

33

fa-idhā فَإِذَا But when jāati جَاءَتِ comes l-ṭāmatu الطَّامَّةُ the Overwhelming Calamity l-kub'rā الْكُبْرَى the great

34

34.

But when there comes the greatest Overwhelming Calamity -

34

yawma يَوْمَ (The) Day yatadhakkaru يَتَذَكَّرُ will remember l-insānu الْإِنْسَانُ man mā مَا what saʿā سَعَى he strove (for)

35

35.

The Day when man will remember that for which he strove,

35

waburrizati وَبُرِّزَتِ And will be made manifest l-jaḥīmu الْجَحِيمُ the Hell-Fire liman لِمَنْ to (him) who yarā يَرَى sees

36

36.

And Hellfire will be exposed for [all] those who see -

36

fa-ammā فَأَمَّا Then as for man مَنْ (him) who ṭaghā طَغَى transgressed

37

37.

So as for he who transgressed

37

waāthara وَءَاثَرَ And preferred l-ḥayata الْحَيَوةَ the life l-dun'yā الدُّنْيَا (of) the world

38

38.

And preferred the life of the world,

38

fa-inna فَإِنَّ Then indeed l-jaḥīma الْجَحِيمَ the Hell-Fire hiya هِيَ it l-mawā الْمَأْوَى (is) the refuge

39

39.

Then indeed, Hellfire will be [his] refuge.

39

wa-ammā وَأَمَّا But as for man مَنْ (him) who khāfa خَافَ feared maqāma مَقَامَ standing rabbihi رَبِّهِ (before) his Lord wanahā وَنَهَى and restrained l-nafsa النَّفْسَ his soul ʿani عَنِ from l-hawā الْهَوَى the vain desires

40

40.

But as for he who feared the position of his Lord and prevented the soul from [unlawful] inclination,

40

fa-inna فَإِنَّ Then indeed l-janata الْجَنَّةَ Paradise hiya هِيَ it (is) l-mawā الْمَأْوَى the refuge

41

41.

Then indeed, Paradise will be [his] refuge.

41

yasalūnaka يَسْءَلُونَكَ They ask you ʿani عَنِ about l-sāʿati السَّاعَةِ the Hour ayyāna أَيَّانَ when mur'sāhā مُرْسَاهَا (is) its arrival

42

42.

They ask you, [O Muhammad], about the Hour: when is its arrival?

42

fīma فِيمَ In what anta أَنْتَ (are) you min مِنْ [of] dhik'rāhā ذِكْرَاهَا (to) mention it

43

43.

In what [position] are you that you should mention it?

43

ilā إِلَى To rabbika رَبِّكَ your Lord muntahāhā مُنْتَهَاهَا (is) its finality

44

44.

To your Lord is its finality.

44

innamā إِنَّمَا Only anta أَنْتَ you mundhiru مُنْذِرُ (are) a warner man مَنْ (for him) who yakhshāhā يَخْشَاهَا fears it

45

45.

You are only a warner for those who fear it.

45

ka-annahum كَأَنَّهُمْ As though they yawma يَوْمَ (the) Day yarawnahā يَرَوْنَهَا they see it lam لَمْ not yalbathū يَلْبَثُوا they had remained illā إِلَّا except ʿashiyyatan عَشِيَّةً an evening aw أَوْ or ḍuḥāhā ضُحَاهَا a morning thereof

46

46.

It will be, on the Day they see it, as though they had not remained [in the world] except for an afternoon or a morning thereof.
