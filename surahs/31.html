alif-Lam-meem الٓمٓ Alif Lam Mim

1

1.

Alif, Lam, Meem.

1

til'ka تِلْكَ These āyātu ءَايَاتُ (are) Verses l-kitābi الْكِتَابِ (of) the Book l-ḥakīmi الْحَكِيمِ the Wise

2

2.

These are verses of the wise Book,

2

hudan هُدًى A guidance waraḥmatan وَرَحْمَةً and a mercy lil'muḥ'sinīna لِلْمُحْسِنِينَ for the good-doers

3

3.

As guidance and mercy for the doers of good

3

alladhīna الَّذِينَ Those who yuqīmūna يُقِيمُونَ establish l-ṣalata الصَّلَوةَ the prayer wayu'tūna وَيُؤْتُونَ and give l-zakata الزَّكَوةَ zakah wahum وَهُمْ and they bil-ākhirati بِالْءَاخِرَةِ in the Hereafter hum هُمْ [they] yūqinūna يُوقِنُونَ believe firmly

4

4.

Who establish prayer and give zakah, and they, of the Hereafter, are certain [in faith].

4

ulāika أُولَئِكَ Those ʿalā عَلَى (are) on hudan هُدًى guidance min مِنْ from rabbihim رَبِّهِمْ their Lord wa-ulāika وَأُولَئِكَ and those humu هُمُ [they] l-muf'liḥūna الْمُفْلِحُونَ (are) the successful

5

5.

Those are on [right] guidance from their Lord, and it is those who are the successful.

5

wamina وَمِنَ And of l-nāsi النَّاسِ the mankind man مَنْ (is he) who yashtarī يَشْتَرِي purchases lahwa لَهْوَ idle tales l-ḥadīthi الْحَدِيثِ idle tales liyuḍilla لِيُضِلَّ to mislead ʿan عَنْ from sabīli سَبِيلِ (the) path l-lahi اللَّهِ (of) Allah bighayri بِغَيْرِ without ʿil'min عِلْمٍ knowledge wayattakhidhahā وَيَتَّخِذَهَا and takes it huzuwan هُزُوًا (in) ridicule ulāika أُولَئِكَ Those lahum لَهُمْ for them ʿadhābun عَذَابٌ (is) a punishment muhīnun مُهِينٌ humiliating

6

6.

And of the people is he who buys the amusement of speech to mislead [others] from the way of Allah without knowledge and who takes it in ridicule. Those will have a humiliating punishment.

6

wa-idhā وَإِذَا And when tut'lā تُتْلَى are recited ʿalayhi عَلَيْهِ to him āyātunā ءَايَاتُنَا Our Verses wallā وَلَّى he turns away mus'takbiran مُسْتَكْبِرًا arrogantly ka-an كَأَنْ as if lam لَمْ not yasmaʿhā يَسْمَعْهَا he (had) heard them ka-anna كَأَنَّ as if fī فِي in udhunayhi أُذُنَيْهِ his ears waqran وَقْرًا (is) deafness fabashir'hu فَبَشِّرْهُ So give him tidings biʿadhābin بِعَذَابٍ of a punishment alīmin أَلِيمٍ painful

7

7.

And when our verses are recited to him, he turns away arrogantly as if he had not heard them, as if there was in his ears deafness. So give him tidings of a painful punishment.

7

inna إِنَّ Indeed alladhīna الَّذِينَ those who āmanū ءَامَنُوا believe waʿamilū وَعَمِلُوا and do l-ṣāliḥāti الصَّالِحَاتِ righteous deeds lahum لَهُمْ for them jannātu جَنَّاتُ (are) Gardens l-naʿīmi النَّعِيمِ (of) Delight

8

8.

Indeed, those who believe and do righteous deeds - for them are the Gardens of Pleasure.

8

khālidīna خَالِدِينَ (To) abide forever fīhā فِيهَا in it waʿda وَعْدَ (The) Promise of Allah l-lahi اللَّهِ (The) Promise of Allah ḥaqqan حَقًّا (is) true wahuwa وَهُوَ And He l-ʿazīzu الْعَزِيزُ (is) the All-Mighty l-ḥakīmu الْحَكِيمُ the All-Wise

9

9.

Wherein they abide eternally; [it is] the promise of Allah [which is] truth. And He is the Exalted in Might, the Wise.

9

khalaqa خَلَقَ He created l-samāwāti السَّمَاوَاتِ the heavens bighayri بِغَيْرِ without ʿamadin عَمَدٍ pillars tarawnahā تَرَوْنَهَا that you see wa-alqā وَأَلْقَى and has cast fī فِي in l-arḍi الْأَرْضِ the earth rawāsiya رَوَاسِيَ firm mountains an أَنْ lest tamīda تَمِيدَ it (might) shake bikum بِكُمْ with you wabatha وَبَثَّ and He dispersed fīhā فِيهَا in it min مِنْ from kulli كُلِّ every dābbatin دَابَّةٍ creature wa-anzalnā وَأَنْزَلْنَا And We sent down mina مِنَ from l-samāi السَّمَاءِ the sky māan مَاءً water fa-anbatnā فَأَنْبَتْنَا then We caused to grow fīhā فِيهَا therein min مِنْ of kulli كُلِّ every zawjin زَوْجٍ kind karīmin كَرِيمٍ noble

10

10.

He created the heavens without pillars that you see and has cast into the earth firmly set mountains, lest it should shift with you, and dispersed therein from every creature. And We sent down rain from the sky and made grow therein [plants] of every noble kind.

10

hādhā هَذَا This khalqu خَلْقُ (is the) creation l-lahi اللَّهِ (of) Allah fa-arūnī فَأَرُونِي So show Me mādhā مَاذَا what khalaqa خَلَقَ have created alladhīna الَّذِينَ those min مِنْ besides Him dūnihi دُونِهِ besides Him bali بَلِ Nay l-ẓālimūna الظَّالِمُونَ the wrongdoers fī فِي (are) in ḍalālin ضَلَالٍ error mubīnin مُبِينٍ clear

11

11.

This is the creation of Allah. So show Me what those other than Him have created. Rather, the wrongdoers are in clear error.

11

walaqad وَلَقَدْ And verily ātaynā ءَاتَيْنَا We gave luq'māna لُقْمَانَ Luqman l-ḥik'mata الْحِكْمَةَ the wisdom ani أَنِ that ush'kur اشْكُرْ Be grateful lillahi لِلَّهِ to Allah waman وَمَنْ And whoever yashkur يَشْكُرْ (is) grateful fa-innamā فَإِنَّمَا then only yashkuru يَشْكُرُ he is grateful linafsihi لِنَفْسِهِ for himself waman وَمَنْ And whoever kafara كَفَرَ (is) ungrateful fa-inna فَإِنَّ then indeed l-laha اللَّهَ Allah ghaniyyun غَنِيٌّ (is) Free of need ḥamīdun حَمِيدٌ Praiseworthy

12

12.

And We had certainly given Luqman wisdom [and said], "Be grateful to Allah." And whoever is grateful is grateful for [the benefit of] himself. And whoever denies [His favor] - then indeed, Allah is Free of need and Praiseworthy.

12

wa-idh وَإِذْ And when qāla قَالَ said luq'mānu لُقْمَانُ Luqman li-ib'nihi لِابْنِهِ to his son wahuwa وَهُوَ while he yaʿiẓuhu يَعِظُهُ (was) instructing him yābunayya يَابُنَيَّ O my son! lā لَا (Do) not tush'rik تُشْرِكْ associate partners bil-lahi بِاللَّهِ with Allah inna إِنَّ Indeed l-shir'ka الشِّرْكَ associating partners laẓul'mun لَظُلْمٌ (is) surely an injustice ʿaẓīmun عَظِيمٌ great

13

13.

And [mention, O Muhammad], when Luqman said to his son while he was instructing him, "O my son, do not associate [anything] with Allah. Indeed, association [with him] is great injustice."

13

wawaṣṣaynā وَوَصَّيْنَا And We have enjoined l-insāna الْإِنْسَانَ (upon) man biwālidayhi بِوَالِدَيْهِ for his parents ḥamalathu حَمَلَتْهُ carried him ummuhu أُمُّهُ his mother wahnan وَهْنًا (in) weakness ʿalā عَلَى upon wahnin وَهْنٍ weakness wafiṣāluhu وَفِصَالُهُ and his weaning fī فِي (is) in ʿāmayni عَامَيْنِ two years ani أَنِ that ush'kur اشْكُرْ Be grateful lī لِي to Me waliwālidayka وَلِوَالِدَيْكَ and to your parents ilayya إِلَيَّ towards Me l-maṣīru الْمَصِيرُ (is) the destination

14

14.

And We have enjoined upon man [care] for his parents. His mother carried him, [increasing her] in weakness upon weakness, and his weaning is in two years. Be grateful to Me and to your parents; to Me is the [final] destination.

14

wa-in وَإِنْ But if jāhadāka جَاهَدَاكَ they strive against you ʿalā عَلَى on an أَنْ that tush'rika تُشْرِكَ you associate partners bī بِي with Me mā مَا what laysa لَيْسَ not laka لَكَ you have bihi بِهِ of it ʿil'mun عِلْمٌ any knowledge falā فَلَا then (do) not tuṭiʿ'humā تُطِعْهُمَا obey both of them waṣāḥib'humā وَصَاحِبْهُمَا But accompany them fī فِي in l-dun'yā الدُّنْيَا the world maʿrūfan مَعْرُوفًا (with) kindness wa-ittabiʿ وَاتَّبِعْ and follow sabīla سَبِيلَ (the) path man مَنْ (of him) who anāba أَنَابَ turns ilayya إِلَيَّ to Me thumma ثُمَّ Then ilayya إِلَيَّ towards Me marjiʿukum مَرْجِعُكُمْ (is) your return fa-unabbi-ukum فَأُنَبِّئُكُمْ then I will inform you bimā بِمَا of what kuntum كُنْتُمْ you used (to) taʿmalūna تَعْمَلُونَ do

15

15.

But if they endeavor to make you associate with Me that of which you have no knowledge, do not obey them but accompany them in [this] world with appropriate kindness and follow the way of those who turn back to Me [in repentance]. Then to Me will be your return, and I will inform you about what you used to do.

15

yābunayya يَابُنَيَّ O my son innahā إِنَّهَا Indeed it in إِنْ if taku تَكُ it be mith'qāla مِثْقَالَ (the) weight ḥabbatin حَبَّةٍ (of) a grain min مِنْ of khardalin خَرْدَلٍ a mustard seed fatakun فَتَكُنْ and it be fī فِي in ṣakhratin صَخْرَةٍ a rock aw أَوْ or fī فِي in l-samāwāti السَّمَاوَاتِ the heavens aw أَوْ or fī فِي in l-arḍi الْأَرْضِ the earth yati يَأْتِ Allah will bring it forth bihā بِهَا Allah will bring it forth l-lahu اللَّهُ Allah will bring it forth inna إِنَّ Indeed l-laha اللَّهَ Allah laṭīfun لَطِيفٌ (is) All-Subtle khabīrun خَبِيرٌ All-Aware

16

16.

[And Luqman said], "O my son, indeed if wrong should be the weight of a mustard seed and should be within a rock or [anywhere] in the heavens or in the earth, Allah will bring it forth. Indeed, Allah is Subtle and Acquainted.

16

yābunayya يَابُنَيَّ O my son aqimi أَقِمِ Establish l-ṣalata الصَّلَوةَ the prayer wamur وَأْمُرْ and enjoin bil-maʿrūfi بِالْمَعْرُوفِ [with] the right wa-in'ha وَانْهَ and forbid ʿani عَنِ from l-munkari الْمُنْكَرِ the wrong wa-iṣ'bir وَاصْبِرْ and be patient ʿalā عَلَى over mā مَا what aṣābaka أَصَابَكَ befalls you inna إِنَّ Indeed dhālika ذَلِكَ that min مِنْ (is) of ʿazmi عَزْمِ the matters requiring determination l-umūri الْأُمُورِ the matters requiring determination

17

17.

O my son, establish prayer, enjoin what is right, forbid what is wrong, and be patient over what befalls you. Indeed, [all] that is of the matters [requiring] determination.

17

walā وَلَا And (do) not tuṣaʿʿir تُصَعِّرْ turn khaddaka خَدَّكَ your cheek lilnnāsi لِلنَّاسِ from men walā وَلَا and (do) not tamshi تَمْشِ walk fī فِي in l-arḍi الْأَرْضِ the earth maraḥan مَرَحًا exultantly inna إِنَّ Indeed l-laha اللَّهَ Allah lā لَا (does) not yuḥibbu يُحِبُّ like kulla كُلَّ every mukh'tālin مُخْتَالٍ self-conceited fakhūrin فَخُورٍ boaster

18

18.

And do not turn your cheek [in contempt] toward people and do not walk through the earth exultantly. Indeed, Allah does not like everyone self-deluded and boastful.

18

wa-iq'ṣid وَاقْصِدْ And be moderate fī فِي in mashyika مَشْيِكَ your pace wa-ugh'ḍuḍ وَاغْضُضْ and lower min مِنْ [of] ṣawtika صَوْتِكَ your voice inna إِنَّ Indeed ankara أَنْكَرَ (the) harshest l-aṣwāti الْأَصْوَاتِ (of all) sounds laṣawtu لَصَوْتُ (is) surely (the) voice l-ḥamīri الْحَمِيرِ (of) the donkeys

19

19.

And be moderate in your pace and lower your voice; indeed, the most disagreeable of sounds is the voice of donkeys."

19

alam أَلَمْ Do not taraw تَرَوْا you see anna أَنَّ that l-laha اللَّهَ Allah sakhara سَخَّرَ has subjected lakum لَكُمْ to you mā مَا whatever fī فِي (is) in l-samāwāti السَّمَاوَاتِ the heavens wamā وَمَا and whatever fī فِي (is) in l-arḍi الْأَرْضِ the earth wa-asbagha وَأَسْبَغَ and amply bestowed ʿalaykum عَلَيْكُمْ upon you niʿamahu نِعَمَهُ His Bounties ẓāhiratan ظَاهِرَةً apparent wabāṭinatan وَبَاطِنَةً and hidden wamina وَمِنَ But of l-nāsi النَّاسِ the people man مَنْ (is he) who yujādilu يُجَادِلُ disputes fī فِي about l-lahi اللَّهِ Allah bighayri بِغَيْرِ without ʿil'min عِلْمٍ knowledge walā وَلَا and not hudan هُدًى guidance walā وَلَا and not kitābin كِتَابٍ a book munīrin مُنِيرٍ enlightening

20

20.

Do you not see that Allah has made subject to you whatever is in the heavens and whatever is in the earth and amply bestowed upon you His favors, [both] apparent and unapparent? But of the people is he who disputes about Allah without knowledge or guidance or an enlightening Book [from Him].

20

wa-idhā وَإِذَا And when qīla قِيلَ it is said lahumu لَهُمُ to them ittabiʿū اتَّبِعُوا Follow mā مَا what anzala أَنْزَلَ Allah (has) revealed l-lahu اللَّهُ Allah (has) revealed qālū قَالُوا they say bal بَلْ Nay nattabiʿu نَتَّبِعُ we will follow mā مَا what wajadnā وَجَدْنَا we found ʿalayhi عَلَيْهِ on it ābāanā ءَابَاءَنَا our forefathers awalaw أَوَلَوْ Even if kāna كَانَ Shaitaan was l-shayṭānu الشَّيْطَانُ Shaitaan was yadʿūhum يَدْعُوهُمْ (to) call them ilā إِلَى to ʿadhābi عَذَابِ (the) punishment l-saʿīri السَّعِيرِ (of) the Blaze

21

21.

And when it is said to them, "Follow what Allah has revealed," they say, "Rather, we will follow that upon which we found our fathers." Even if Satan was inviting them to the punishment of the Blaze?

21

waman وَمَنْ And whoever yus'lim يُسْلِمْ submits wajhahu وَجْهَهُ his face ilā إِلَى to l-lahi اللَّهِ Allah wahuwa وَهُوَ while he muḥ'sinun مُحْسِنٌ (is) a good- doer faqadi فَقَدِ then indeed is'tamsaka اسْتَمْسَكَ he has grasped bil-ʿur'wati بِالْعُرْوَةِ the handhold l-wuth'qā الْوُثْقَى the most trustworthy wa-ilā وَإِلَى And to l-lahi اللَّهِ Allah ʿāqibatu عَاقِبَةُ (is the) end l-umūri الْأُمُورِ (of) the matters

22

22.

And whoever submits his face to Allah while he is a doer of good - then he has grasped the most trustworthy handhold. And to Allah will be the outcome of [all] matters.

22

23.

And whoever has disbelieved - let not his disbelief grieve you. To Us is their return, and We will inform them of what they did. Indeed, Allah is Knowing of that within the breasts.

24.

We grant them enjoyment for a little; then We will force them to a massive punishment.

25.

And if you asked them, "Who created the heavens and earth?" they would surely say, "Allah." Say, "[All] praise is [due] to Allah "; but most of them do not know.

26.

To Allah belongs whatever is in the heavens and earth. Indeed, Allah is the Free of need, the Praiseworthy.

27

27.

And if whatever trees upon the earth were pens and the sea [was ink], replenished thereafter by seven [more] seas, the words of Allah would not be exhausted. Indeed, Allah is Exalted in Might and Wise.

27

Your creation and your resurrection will not be but as that of a single soul. Indeed, Allah is Hearing and Seeing.

29.

31.

Do you not see that ships sail through the sea by the favor of Allah that He may show you of His signs? Indeed in that are signs for everyone patient and grateful.

Indeed, Allah [alone] has knowledge of the Hour and sends down the rain and knows what is in the wombs. And no soul perceives what it will earn tomorrow, and no soul perceives in what land it will die. Indeed, Allah is Knowing and Acquainted.
