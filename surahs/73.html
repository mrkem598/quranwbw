yāayyuhā يَاأَيُّهَا O you l-muzamilu الْمُزَّمِّلُ who wraps himself!

1

1.

O you who wraps himself [in clothing],

1

qumi قُمِ Stand al-layla اللَّيْلَ (in) the night illā إِلَّا except qalīlan قَلِيلًا a little

2

2.

Arise [to pray] the night, except for a little -

2

niṣ'fahu نِصْفَهُ Half of it awi أَوِ or unquṣ انْقُصْ lessen min'hu مِنْهُ from it qalīlan قَلِيلًا a little

3

3.

Half of it - or subtract from it a little

3

aw أَوْ Or zid زِدْ add ʿalayhi عَلَيْهِ to it warattili وَرَتِّلِ and recite l-qur'āna الْقُرْءَانَ the Quran tartīlan تَرْتِيلًا (with) measured rhythmic recitation

4

4.

Or add to it, and recite the Qur'an with measured recitation.

4

innā إِنَّا Indeed We sanul'qī سَنُلْقِي will cast ʿalayka عَلَيْكَ upon you qawlan قَوْلًا a Word thaqīlan ثَقِيلًا heavy

5

5.

Indeed, We will cast upon you a heavy word.

5

inna إِنَّ Indeed nāshi-ata نَاشِئَةَ (the) rising al-layli اللَّيْلِ (at) the night hiya هِيَ it ashaddu أَشَدُّ (is) very hard waṭan وَطْئًا and most potent wa-aqwamu وَأَقْوَمُ and more suitable qīlan قِيلًا (for) Word

6

6.

Indeed, the hours of the night are more effective for concurrence [of heart and tongue] and more suitable for words.

6

inna إِنَّ Indeed laka لَكَ for you fī فِي in l-nahāri النَّهَارِ the day sabḥan سَبْحًا (is) occupation ṭawīlan طَوِيلًا prolonged

7

7.

Indeed, for you by day is prolonged occupation.

7

wa-udh'kuri وَاذْكُرِ And remember is'ma اسْمَ (the) name rabbika رَبِّكَ (of) your Lord watabattal وَتَبَتَّلْ and devote yourself ilayhi إِلَيْهِ to Him tabtīlan تَبْتِيلًا (with) devotion

8

8.

And remember the name of your Lord and devote yourself to Him with [complete] devotion.

8

rabbu رَبُّ (The) Lord l-mashriqi الْمَشْرِقِ (of) the east wal-maghribi وَالْمَغْرِبِ and the west; lā لَا (there is) no ilāha إِلَهَ god illā إِلَّا except huwa هُوَ Him fa-ittakhidh'hu فَاتَّخِذْهُ so take Him wakīlan وَكِيلًا (as) Disposer of Affairs

9

9.

[He is] the Lord of the East and the West; there is no deity except Him, so take Him as Disposer of [your] affairs.

9

wa-iṣ'bir وَاصْبِرْ And be patient ʿalā عَلَى over mā مَا what yaqūlūna يَقُولُونَ they say wa-uh'jur'hum وَاهْجُرْهُمْ and avoid them hajran هَجْرًا an avoidance jamīlan جَمِيلًا gracious

10

10.

And be patient over what they say and avoid them with gracious avoidance.

10

wadharnī وَذَرْنِي And leave Me wal-mukadhibīna وَالْمُكَذِّبِينَ and the deniers ulī أُولِي possessors l-naʿmati النَّعْمَةِ (of) the ease wamahhil'hum وَمَهِّلْهُمْ and allow them respite qalīlan قَلِيلًا a little

11

11.

And leave Me with [the matter of] the deniers, those of ease [in life], and allow them respite a little.

11

inna إِنَّ Indeed ladaynā لَدَيْنَا with Us ankālan أَنْكَالًا (are) shackles wajaḥīman وَجَحِيمًا and burning fire

12

12.

Indeed, with Us [for them] are shackles and burning fire

12

waṭaʿāman وَطَعَامًا And food dhā ذَا that ghuṣṣatin غُصَّةٍ chokes waʿadhāban وَعَذَابًا and a punishment alīman أَلِيمًا painful

13

13.

And food that chokes and a painful punishment -

13

yawma يَوْمَ On (the) Day tarjufu تَرْجُفُ will quake l-arḍu الْأَرْضُ the earth wal-jibālu وَالْجِبَالُ and the mountains wakānati وَكَانَتِ and will become l-jibālu الْجِبَالُ the mountains kathīban كَثِيبًا a heap of sand mahīlan مَهِيلًا pouring down

14

14.

On the Day the earth and the mountains will convulse and the mountains will become a heap of sand pouring down.

14

innā إِنَّا Indeed We arsalnā أَرْسَلْنَا [We] have sent ilaykum إِلَيْكُمْ to you rasūlan رَسُولًا a Messenger shāhidan شَاهِدًا (as) a witness ʿalaykum عَلَيْكُمْ upon you kamā كَمَا as arsalnā أَرْسَلْنَا We sent ilā إِلَى to fir'ʿawna فِرْعَوْنَ Firaun rasūlan رَسُولًا a Messenger

15

15.

Indeed, We have sent to you a Messenger as a witness upon you just as We sent to Pharaoh a messenger.

15

faʿaṣā فَعَصَى But disobeyed fir'ʿawnu فِرْعَوْنُ Firaun l-rasūla الرَّسُولَ the Messenger fa-akhadhnāhu فَأَخَذْنَاهُ so We seized him akhdhan أَخْذًا (with) a seizure wabīlan وَبِيلًا ruinous

16

16.

But Pharaoh disobeyed the messenger, so We seized him with a ruinous seizure.

16

fakayfa فَكَيْفَ Then how tattaqūna تَتَّقُونَ will you guard yourselves in إِنْ if kafartum كَفَرْتُمْ you disbelieve yawman يَوْمًا a Day yajʿalu يَجْعَلُ (that) will make l-wil'dāna الْوِلْدَانَ the children shīban شِيبًا gray-headed

17

17.

Then how can you fear, if you disbelieve, a Day that will make the children white- haired?

17

al-samāu السَّمَاءُ The heaven munfaṭirun مُنْفَطِرٌ (will) break apart bihi بِهِ therefrom kāna كَانَ is waʿduhu وَعْدُهُ His Promise mafʿūlan مَفْعُولًا to be fulfilled

18

18.

The heaven will break apart therefrom; ever is His promise fulfilled.

18

inna إِنَّ Indeed hādhihi هَذِهِ this tadhkiratun تَذْكِرَةٌ (is) a Reminder faman فَمَنْ then whoever shāa شَاءَ wills ittakhadha اتَّخَذَ let him take ilā إِلَى to rabbihi رَبِّهِ his Lord sabīlan سَبِيلًا a way

19

19.

Indeed, this is a reminder, so whoever wills may take to his Lord a way.

19

20

20.

Indeed, your Lord knows, [O Muhammad], that you stand [in prayer] almost two thirds of the night or half of it or a third of it, and [so do] a group of those with you. And Allah determines [the extent of] the night and the day. He has known that you [Muslims] will not be able to do it and has turned to you in forgiveness, so recite what is easy [for you] of the Qur'an. He has known that there will be among you those who are ill and others traveling throughout the land seeking [something] of the bounty of Allah and others fighting for the cause of Allah. So recite what is easy from it and establish prayer and give zakah and loan Allah a goodly loan. And whatever good you put forward for yourselves - you will find it with Allah. It is better and greater in reward. And seek forgiveness of Allah. Indeed, Allah is Forgiving and Merciful.
